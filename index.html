Dr. Alexander Fleming

The man who discovered penicilin

Here is a time line of Dr. Alexander Fleming's life

  • 1881 - Born in Darvel, East Ayrshire, Scotland.
  • 1906 - He quialified with MBBS degree from St Mary's Hospital Medical School in Paddington with distinction.
  • 1908 - He gained a BSc degree with gold medal in Bacteriology, and became a lecturer at St Mary's until 1914.
  • 1918 - He returned to St Mary's Hospital after served throughout World War I in the Royal Army Medical Corps being Commissioned lieutenant in 1914 and promoted captain in 1917.
  • 1928 - He discovered the world's first broadly effective antibiotic wich he named peniciline. He also was elected Professor of Bacteriology of the University of London the same year.
  • 1943 - Despite it effectiveness, it wasn't until this year when he published an important clinical case called "The Lancet" that the scientist community payed the deserved atention to his discovery.
  • 1995 - Fleming dies at his home in London at the age of 73.
